USES : Glyphosate is one of the most widely used herbicides with applications :
agriculture, forestry, industrial weed control, lawn, garden, and aquatic environments.
Sites with the largest glyphosate use include soybeans, field corn, pasture and hay.

Why is it an advantage to make plants resistant to glyphosate?

This glyphosate resistance enables farmers to wipe out most weeds from the fields without damaging their crops.
Glyphosate inhibits plant growth by blocking an enzyme known as EPSP synthase, Roundup, can trigger loss of biodiversity, making ecosystems more vulnerable to pollution and climate change,
glyphosate may cause eye or skin irritation.
